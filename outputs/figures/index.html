This report analyzes 381,999 Yelp reviews across 8,203 coffee-chain locations in 13 U.S. states from 2017 to 2021. The primary subject is Starbucks, benchmarked against Dunkin' — its nearest national competitor with 6,866 reviews in the dataset — and the broader independent café market. Sentiment scoring via VADER, rule-based topic tagging, and reviewer segmentation are applied throughout to surface actionable patterns in customer voice data.

2.90★ vs. 2.05★ Brand Gap

Starbucks outperforms Dunkin' by 0.85 stars on average across all measured states, though both chains trail the independent café benchmark of 3.87★.

38.4% Service Topic Share

Service dominates the topic landscape, accounting for 38.4% of all tagged reviews and carrying the lowest positive rate of any topic for both major chains.

24.0% One-Star Rate — Starbucks

One-star reviews account for nearly a quarter of all Starbucks ratings — a bimodal distribution driven by polarized customer experiences, not generalized dissatisfaction.

+0.09★ Weekend Premium

Weekend visits are rated 0.09 stars higher on average than weekdays, pointing to a measurable experience difference likely driven by staffing and pace-of-service variation.

NB 05

Review Volume Trends

Review activity across all brands peaked in 2019, followed by a 44% contraction in 2020 attributable to COVID-19 pandemic restrictions on in-store dining. Volume partially recovered in 2021 but remained well below pre-pandemic levels — a pattern consistent across all brand categories. The dataset spans 13 states and 508 cities, with Arizona and Nevada representing the largest share of review volume. Nevada also posts the highest average star rating of any state in the sample.

NB 06

Rating Distribution

The star distribution for Starbucks is bimodal — with a concentration of both 1-star and 5-star reviews — indicating a polarized customer base rather than uniform moderate satisfaction. The 1-star tier accounts for 24.0% of all Starbucks ratings, while 5-star reviews comprise 38.2%. VADER sentiment scores confirm a gradual decline in average compound sentiment from 2019 onward, coinciding with COVID-19 disruption and subsequent post-pandemic service quality pressure.

NB 07

Voice of Customer

Reviews were tagged across seven operational topics — Service, Food Quality, Ambiance, Wait Time, Price, Cleanliness, and General — using rule-based keyword matching. Service dominates by volume (38.4% of tagged reviews) and carries the lowest positive rate of any topic for both Starbucks and Dunkin'. Word frequency analysis confirms that positive reviews center on product quality and brand loyalty, while critical reviews are driven overwhelmingly by service and wait-time grievances.

Key Finding

Starbucks outperforms Dunkin' across every measured topic, with the largest gap in Food Quality (63.2% vs. 41.5% positive rate). Service remains the weakest dimension for both brands — and the highest-volume topic — making it the primary lever for customer experience improvement.

NB 08

Reviewer Segmentation

Yelp reviewers were segmented into four activity tiers: Casual (1–5 reviews), Regular (6–25), Power (26–100), and Elite (101+). Higher-activity reviewers apply more critical standards: Elite reviewers average 3.12★ versus 3.87★ for Casual reviewers on Starbucks. Power-tier reviews are also significantly more detailed, averaging 86 words per review compared to 52 for Casual — a signal that engaged users invest more in articulating their experience.

NB 10

Time Pattern Analysis

Rating patterns were analyzed by day of week and month of year to identify temporal variation in customer experience. Weekends (Saturday–Sunday) consistently produce higher average ratings than weekdays, with a mean premium of +0.09★ for Starbucks. Monthly patterns show a mild downward drift in summer months (July–August), potentially linked to higher in-store traffic and corresponding wait time pressure during peak seasons.
